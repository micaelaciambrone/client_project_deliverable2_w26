Navigating Your Path from Campus to Career

Job Searching, Made Strategic

A successful job search is more than submitting applications—it’s about building a strategy, staying organized, and leveraging the resources available to you as a UMSI student. Whether you’re exploring roles, applying for internships, or preparing for full-time positions, this page is designed to support you at every stage of the process.

From identifying opportunities and preparing applications to connecting with employers and alumni, UMSI provides tools and guidance to help you move confidently from campus to career. Use the resources below to stay on track, refine your approach, and make informed decisions throughout your search.

Craft Your Story

Work with our advisors to build a resume that stands out to top-tier recruiters. Our team specialized in UMSI-specific career paths helps you translate your academic projects into professional achievements that catch the eye of hiring managers.

Meet Your Future Employers

Attend our premier seasonal career fairs, hosted at iconic venues like the Michigan Union and the Duderstadt Center. These events provide an exclusive platform to connect with over 500 global companies, ranging from Fortune 500 industry leaders to innovative tech startups. Whether you are looking for a summer internship or a full-time leadership position, these fairs are your gateway to a world-class professional network.

Job Search FAQ

  • When should I start applying for jobs or internships?
    Many employers begin recruiting several months in advance. Start exploring opportunities early and check CareerLink regularly for new postings.
  • Where can I find opportunities relevant to UMSI students?
    UMSI CareerLink, LinkedIn, and employer career pages are great places to start. Career fairs and networking events can also connect you directly with recruiters.
  • How many applications should I submit?
    Focus on submitting thoughtful, tailored applications rather than applying broadly without customization. Quality often leads to better results than quantity.
  • What should I do if I do not hear back from an employer?
    It is normal for hiring timelines to vary. Continue applying to other opportunities and consider following up politely if contact information is available.
